U-19 Asia Rugby Championship

The 2018 U-19 Asia Rugby Championship is an international rugby union competition for Under 20 national teams in Asia. The winners in Asia Rugby U19 Championship will secure a berth at the 2019 World Rugby Under 20 Trophy.

Chinese Taipei

Taiwan’s rugby team beat South Korea 7:5, Saturday, December 15, after the Koreans beat the championship favorite Hong Kong on the first day of the Asia Rugby Under 19 championship Wednesday.

The 4-team competition, hosted in Taipei between December 12 and December 16 saw Taiwan, playing under the moniker “Chinese Taipei” beat Sri Lanka 18-15, while Korea won over five-time defending champions Hong Kong 17-13 on the first day of competition Wednesday.

Hong Kong

Hong Kong beat Chinese Taipei 38-5 to claim the Asia Rugby U19 Championship on Tuesday.In the final match of the four-team round-robin competition, Hong Kong scored six tries to the hosts one and claimed a bonus point victory.

Toby Jurecka scored two tries while Paul Altier scored 13 points in a win that secured their victory in the competition and in doing so ensured they qualified for next year’s World Rugby U20 Trophy in Brazil.

South Korea

December 15, saw Taiwan defeating what was considered a stronger South Korean team, in front of a 3,000-strong home crowd at the Taipei Municipal Stadium, while Hong Kong trounced Sri Lanka 48-15.December 18, the final round of the competition will see Sri Lanka face Korea, while the home side will play against Hong Kong.If the Taiwan team manages to defeat Hong Kong on Tuesday, they will advance to the Under 20 World Rugby Trophy campaign for the first time in history.
